Sharks are a group of elasmobranch fish characterized by a cartilaginous skeleton, five to seven gill slits on the sides of the head, pectoral fins that are not fused to the head, and a large tail fin. They are found in all seas and are common to coastal waters. Their bodies are adapted for a predatory lifestyle, with many species being apex predators in their ecosystems. Sharks have been historically vilified and feared, but they play a crucial role in maintaining the health of marine environments.
